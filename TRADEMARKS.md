This policy governs the use of the names, logos, and other brand assets

## 2. Permitted Uses (No Permission Required)

You may use the Marks without contacting the Owner in the following ways:

- **Referring to the project** in articles, blog posts, books, talks,
  reviews, comparisons, tutorials, and similar editorial content,
  provided the use is truthful and not misleading.
- **Indicating compatibility or interoperability** of your own product
  with Stellar Grooves (e.g., "works with Stellar Grooves", "imports
  from Stellar Grooves"), provided your product is not falsely
  represented as endorsed by, affiliated with, or originating from
  the Stellar Grooves project or the Owner.
- **Internal use** within your organization, including running an
  unmodified copy of the software for your own business purposes.
- **Personal and non-commercial use** of the unmodified software,
  including referring to it by its name in personal projects and
  social media.

In all permitted uses, the Marks must be used as adjectives, not nouns
or verbs (e.g., "the Stellar Grooves application", not "a Stellar
Grooves" or "to Stellar Groove something").

The following uses require advance written permission from the Owner:

- Using the Marks in the **name of a product, service, company, domain,

## 4. Forks and Derivative Works

The AGPL-3.0-or-later license permits anyone to fork, modify, and
redistribute the source code. However:

- Forks distributed publicly **must be renamed**. They may not use
  "Stellar Grooves", confusingly similar names, or the project's logo
  in a way that implies they are the original or official version.
- Forks **may** describe themselves accurately as "based on Stellar
  Grooves" or "a fork of Stellar Grooves", consistent with Section 2.
- Forks **must** remove or replace any project branding (logo, wordmark)
  in user-visible UI surfaces if the fork is made publicly available.

This requirement protects users from confusion about which project is
the upstream and reduces support burden caused by misattribution.

Owner.

If you need to use a logo or screenshot in a context not covered by
Section 2, request permission per Section 3.

The Owner reserves the right to update this policy at any time and to
take action to protect the Marks against misuse. Failure to enforce a
particular use does not constitute a waiver of rights against future
uses.

This policy is intended to give the community clear guidance, not to
be adversarial. If you are unsure whether a planned use is permitted,
ask — most reasonable requests are granted.
